# 📝 Paper Notes

A personal knowledge base for tracking, summarizing, and annotating research papers — focused on Machine Learning, Deep Learning, NLP, and related areas.

**Author:** Nikolaos Roufas

---

## Overview

This repository contains structured notes on research papers I read as part of my undergraduate studies and independent research in Computer Science. The goal is to maintain a clean, searchable record of papers across topics like LLMs, RAG systems, interpretability, and more.

---

## Structure

```
papernotes/
├── nlp/               # Papers on NLP and language models
├── deep-learning/     # General deep learning papers
├── rag/               # Retrieval-Augmented Generation
├── interpretability/  # Model interpretability and explainability
└── misc/              # Other relevant papers
```

Each note typically includes:
- **Title & Authors**
- **Venue / Year**
- **Summary** — what the paper proposes and why it matters
- **Key contributions**
- **Limitations / open questions**
- **Personal notes** — thoughts, connections to other work, or questions for further reading

---

## Topics Covered

- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
- Natural Language Processing (NLP)
- Deep Learning architectures
- Model interpretability

---

## Purpose

This is primarily a personal learning tool, but it may be useful to others studying similar topics. Notes are written to be clear and concise — not full summaries, but enough to recall the key ideas and revisit the paper with context.
